# Section 2 - Key concepts

- Systemic Racism: Systemic racism is racial discrimination in all the systems of our society. These systems include the our society's structure as well as its institutions, and within them white supremacy is upheld.

- Education: One of the solutions to combat systemic racism is education. By educating ourselves about the black and indigenous history, we are able to see the racism that surround us.

- Humility: Another way to combat systemic racism is humility. When talking about race, being aware of our white fragility and accepting the feeling of discomfort are humble ways needed to accept Canada's racist history.

- White supremacy: The idea that the white racial group is superior to other racial groups. Because white people have been thought to be superior to people of color in the past, Canada was created through a racist system that still persists today. White supremacy in the past led to the systemic racism that we know today.
